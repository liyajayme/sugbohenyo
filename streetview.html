Historical Landmarks of Cebu

Cebu—often called the “Cradle of Christianity”—is packed with heritage sites that shaped Philippine history. Here are five landmark stops with quick facts and Street View previews.

01

Fort San Pedro (Cebu City)

Fort San Pedro is the oldest and smallest fort in the Philippines, built in 1565 under the leadership of Miguel López de Legazpi. It was constructed using stone and coral, with the help of Spanish and local workers.

The fort served as a military defense structure against pirates and foreign invaders during the Spanish colonial period. Over time, it was also used as a prison, a barracks for soldiers, and even a school. Today, it is preserved as a museum where visitors can learn about Cebu’s colonial history through artifacts and exhibits.

02

Basilica Minore del Santo Niño (Cebu City)

The Basilica Minore del Santo Niño is the oldest Roman Catholic church in the Philippines, established in 1565. It was built on the site where Spanish soldiers discovered the image of the Santo Niño after a fire.

The image was originally given by Ferdinand Magellan to Queen Juana in 1521, marking the beginning of Christianity in the Philippines. Today, the basilica is an important religious center and attracts thousands of devotees, especially during the annual Sinulog Festival.

03

Magellan’s Cross (Cebu City)

Magellan's Cross is one of the most famous historical landmarks in Cebu. It was planted in 1521 by Ferdinand Magellan upon his arrival in the Philippines.

The cross symbolizes the introduction of Christianity in the country. The original cross is believed to be encased within the wooden structure seen today, as it was protected from people who once chipped off parts of it, believing it had healing powers.

04

Mactan Shrine (Lapu-Lapu City)

The Mactan Shrine is a historical site that commemorates the Battle of Mactan in 1521. This was the battle where Datu Lapu-Lapu and his warriors defeated Ferdinand Magellan.

The shrine features monuments dedicated to both Lapu-Lapu and Magellan. Lapu-Lapu is recognized as the first Filipino hero for resisting foreign colonization. The site serves as a reminder of Filipino bravery and independence.

05

Heritage of Cebu Monument (Parian, Cebu City)

The Heritage of Cebu Monument is a large sculpture located in Parian, Cebu City. It was created by artist Eduardo Castrillo and showcases important events in Cebu’s history.

The monument includes scenes such as the arrival of Magellan, the baptism of Rajah Humabon, and the devotion to the Santo Niño. Made from concrete, bronze, and steel, it stands as a visual summary of Cebu’s heritage.
